Who am I?

Even though I've recently graduated, I still consider myself to be a life-long learner. Discovering new things and developing my skillset plays a big part in who I am. I don't consider myself the best or smartest programmer the world has to offer, but hard-working and tenacious are words often used to describe me. I like to focus on my task and get things done.

My dream has always been to develop myself into someone who is useful. I want to provide value whereever this journey takes me. A big goal for me is to prove myself and stand proudly amongst my peers; I want to be recognized for my efforts and admired for my ability within the craft. A lofty goal, I know, but it's an honest one.

So while l know that I'm just a growing bud in this industry, it won't stop me. It only makes me more excited to see what my future has in store for me.

Philosophy

First and foremost, I am a problem solver. I help real people solve real problems. To do that, I always have an array of tools that are ready to use. My favourite? Web Technologies. At the moment, I am knee-deep in and working my way through the in ASP.NET world.

Secondly, I want to fix things. The unfortunate reality is that things break all the time. My focus as a developer is to make sure the things I build are maintained and well-documented. This ensures longevity of my product and less headache for anyone else who looks at my code.
